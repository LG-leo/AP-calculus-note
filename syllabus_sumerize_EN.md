## Part 3: Course Positioning

### 3.1 Course Goals

> AP Calculus AB and BC focus on **students' understanding of calculus concepts** and provide **experience with methods and applications**. Through the use of **three Big Ideas** (Change, Limits, and Analysis of Functions), each course becomes a cohesive whole, rather than a collection of unrelated topics.

**Multi-representational approach**: Concepts, results, and problems are expressed **graphically, numerically, analytically, and verbally**. Students must use definitions and theorems to build arguments and justify conclusions.

### 3.2 AB vs. BC Comparison

| Aspect | AP Calculus AB | AP Calculus BC |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| **College Equivalent** | First semester college calculus | First + second semester college calculus |
| **Number of Units** | 8 units | 10 units (all AB content + 2 BC-only units) |
| **BC-Only Topics** | — | Parametric equations, polar coordinates, vector-valued functions, infinite sequences & series, integration by parts, partial fractions, improper integrals, Euler's method, logistic models, arc length, etc. |
| **Prerequisites** | 4 years of secondary math (algebra, geometry, trigonometry, analytic geometry, elementary functions) | Same as AB + basic familiarity with sequences/series and parametric/polar equations |

### 3.3 Required Prerequisite Knowledge

Students must be proficient with: **linear, polynomial, rational, exponential, logarithmic, trigonometric, inverse trigonometric, and piecewise-defined functions**. They must understand function properties (domain, range, odd/even, periodicity, symmetry, zeros, intercepts, increasing/decreasing), the unit circle definition of sine and cosine, and trigonometric function values at key angles.

## Part 4: The Three Big Ideas

### 🌟 Big Idea 1: CHANGE (CHA)

> Using **derivatives** to describe rates of change of one variable with respect to another, or using **definite integrals** to describe the net change in one variable over an interval of another, allows students to understand change in a variety of contexts.

**Central concept**: The **Fundamental Theorem of Calculus** connects integration and differentiation — a central idea in AP Calculus.

**Essential question**: Can change occur at an instant?

### 🌟 Big Idea 2: LIMITS (LIM)

> Beginning with a discrete model and then considering the consequences of a limiting case allows us to model real-world behavior and to discover important ideas, definitions, formulas, and theorems in calculus: continuity, differentiation, integration, and series (BC only).

**Essential question**: How does knowing the value of a limit, or that a limit does not exist, help you make sense of interesting features of functions and their graphs?

### 🌟 Big Idea 3: ANALYSIS OF FUNCTIONS (FUN)

> Calculus allows us to analyze the behaviors of functions by relating limits to differentiation, integration, and infinite series and relating each of these concepts to the others.

**Essential question**: How do we close loopholes so that a conclusion about a function is always true?

## Part 10: AB vs. BC — Key Differences Summary

| Dimension | AB | BC |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| **Units** | 8 (Unit 1–8) | 10 (Unit 1–10) |
| **Total Periods** | ~118–127 | ~107–118 |
| **Key Theorems** | MVT, EVT, IVT, FTC | Same + Ratio Test, Integral Test, Comparison Tests, etc. |
| **Integration Techniques** | Substitution, long division, completing the square | All AB + integration by parts, partial fractions, improper integrals |
| **Differential Equations** | Slope fields, separation of variables, exponential models | All AB + Euler's method, logistic models |
| **Special Topics** | — | Parametric equations, polar coordinates, vector-valued functions, arc length |
| **Series** | — | Geometric series, convergence tests (7 types), Taylor/Maclaurin series, Lagrange error bound |
| **Difficulty** | First semester college calculus equivalent | Both first & second semester college calculus equivalent |
| **Highest Weight Units** | Unit 6 (Integration) 17–20% | Unit 6 (Integration) 17–20% + Unit 10 (Series) 17–18% |
